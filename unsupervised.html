At Accenture, I chose unsupervised ML models for anomaly detection because our fraud patterns were constantly evolving, making it difficult to label historical data accurately. Supervised methods require a large, well-labeled dataset of both normal and fraudulent transactions, which we lacked. Unsupervised models, on the other hand, learn the normal behavior patterns from the data itself without needing labeled examples of fraud. They flag any data points that deviate significantly from these patterns as potential anomalies. This approach is more adaptable to emerging fraud tactics."
