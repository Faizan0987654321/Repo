To quantify the reduction in false positives, we used a year's worth of transaction data as our baseline, manually reviewed by our fraud experts. This baseline had a false positive rate of about 7.2%, significantly burdening our review team. We then ran this data through our new iForest model, followed by manual review. The model's false positive rate was around 5.2%, a 27.78% reduction (rounded to 28% in my resume). This improvement allowed our team to focus more on true threats, preventing an estimated $500,000 in potential fraud losses annually."
